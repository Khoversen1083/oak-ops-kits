# The 3-Sentence Email

## What you're building

A three-sentence cold email that gets read on a phone in 5 seconds. One specific business problem, one specific outcome, one ask. The hook that turns research into replies.

## Before you start

- Locked niche, scored ICP, 6-layer brief on each A-tier prospect
- A warmed sending inbox (two weeks minimum)
- The Cold Email v1 template from Resources

## Steps

1. **Write the subject line.** Reference something specific they posted or hired for. `[firstname], quick question on your [pain area]` pulls 2-3x the open rate of generic openers.
2. **Sentence 1 - the observation.** Name the specific manual process you believe they're doing. Pulled from the research brief.
3. **Sentence 2 - the outcome.** Reference a similar build, in their language. "Hours back, errors cut," not "AI-powered platform." Use only real, approved proof. If you don't have it yet, soften: "we found a weekly reporting workflow that was eating hours."
4. **Sentence 3 - the ask.** "Open to a 15-minute call this week or next?" Nothing else.
5. **Send Tuesday to Thursday, 7-9am their local time.** Owner-operators check email early.
6. After sending, log "Sent" in the pipeline. Set follow-up 1 reminder for day 3. Do not check the inbox four times an hour. Spend that time sourcing the next 25.

## The template

```
Subject: [firstname], quick question on [specific operational area]

Hey [firstname],

I help [niche] owners automate the manual work eating their team's time, things like [pain hypothesis from research brief].

A recent build for a similar-sized [niche] turned [manual process] into something that runs on its own. Happy to walk through how it works.

Open to a 15-minute call this week or next?

[your first name]
```

## Anti-patterns

- "I came across your company and was impressed by..." Every cold email opens this way.
- "I'd love to share how AI is transforming [industry]." They hear AI 50 times a day.
- "We're a leading provider of..." They don't care.
- Naming tools. They want hours back, not technology.
