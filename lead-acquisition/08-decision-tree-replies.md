# Decision Tree Per Reply Type

## What you're building

A response playbook for every reply type. Reply within four hours business hours. Never pitch in the reply. Get the call first. One link, one ask.

## Before you start

- Cold email + 3-touch sequence running
- Pipeline tracker open with status column
- Calendly link ready (Playbook Intro, 15 min)
- The Reply Handler template from Resources

## Steps

1. **"Yes, let's talk"** - send the Calendly, add a prep ask ("which manual processes are slowing your team most right now"), set status to **booked**.
2. **"What exactly do you do?"** - short version, one example, end with the ask. Do NOT send the Calendly yet. Make them confirm interest. Set status to **engaged**.
3. **"What's it cost?"** - answer the range ($1K-2.5K setup, $300-600/mo retainer). Add: exact number depends on what we'd build, which is why the 15-min call is best. Send the Calendly. Status **pricing-shared**.
4. **"We already work with someone"** - not a no. Reposition as additive. "Where I usually fit is the workflows that kind of work but eat hours every week." Offer 15 min for an honest read. Status **engaged**.
5. **"Not interested"** - take it cleanly. "If anything changes, my email's the same." Status **soft-no, re-engage 90 days**. Half come back when the pain gets worse.
6. **"Wrong person, talk to [other contact]"** - gold. Pre-warmed referral. Thank them, then email the new contact and reference who sent you in sentence one.

## Rules

- Reply within 4 hours business hours, same day minimum.
- Never pitch in the reply. Get the call.
- One link, one ask. If they want more, they'll ask.
- Save every no with a date. Re-engage in 90 days with a new result.
- If a reply throws you, post in the community before responding. Better to wait 6 hours and respond well than reply fast and badly.
