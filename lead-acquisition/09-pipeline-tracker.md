# One Sheet, Every Prospect, Reply to Calendar

## What you're building

A single pipeline tracker that owns every prospect across every tier. Auto-flags who needs follow-up today. Turns a reply into a booked call without losing the thread.

## Before you start

- Cold email + 3-touch sequence running
- Reply handler dialed
- Calendly Playbook Intro live (15 min)
- The Pipeline Tracker downloaded from Resources

## Steps

1. **Open Pipeline Tracker.** Every prospect goes in, no exceptions. Tracking in your head is how deals go cold.
2. **Required columns:** company, owner, email, LinkedIn, city/state, source, ICP score + tier, pain hypothesis (one sentence), send dates (first + FU1/2/3), reply date + type, status, next action + next-action date, deal value + retainer.
3. **Two auto columns earn their keep:** days since last touch, and color-coded status. Amber at 3 days untouched, red at 8, green when booked, gray strike for kills.
4. **Status flow:** awaiting reply → awaiting FU1 (day 3) → FU2 (day 8) → breakup (day 14) → closed-no-response. On reply, jump to: booked, discovered, pilot-scoped, closed-won, closed-lost, soft-no (re-engage 90d), hard-no, do-not-contact.
5. **Booking a call.** One Calendly link, never three options. Add the prep ask. Log the event on their row. Status to booked.
6. **The night before the call,** re-open their 6-layer brief. Walk in knowing their business.

## The stuck signal

Pipeline has 50+ rows but nothing reaches booked. One of two things is wrong:

- The cold email is broken. Rewrite the hook.
- The niche is wrong. Go back to the niche lesson.
