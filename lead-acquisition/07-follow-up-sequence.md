# The 3-Touch Cadence

## What you're building

A three-touch follow-up sequence after the cold email. All in the same thread. Stops at day 14. 5-10% of replies come from the breakup, because owners hate to feel like they ghosted someone.

## Before you start

- Cold email v1 sent on day 0
- Pipeline tracker open with day 3, 8, 14 reminders set
- The Follow-Up Sequence template from Resources

## Steps

1. **Day 0 - cold email.** Already sent.
2. **Day 3 - Follow-up 1.** Concrete proof. "Same kind of business, similar size. They had [process] eating [time]. We built [one-line]. Now it runs overnight." Reply to your own send. Same thread.
3. **Day 8 - Follow-up 2.** Trade the ask. Instead of demanding their time, ask them to confirm or correct your hypothesis. Add a 30-second Loom offer as low-friction value.
4. **Day 14 - Follow-up 3, the breakup.** "Last note from me, don't want to clog your inbox. If [pain] isn't a priority, totally fair. If it becomes one, my email's the same."
5. **Day 14+** - stop. Re-engage in 90 days only if you have shipped a relevant new result.

## Rules

- Same thread every time. Never start a new one.
- No new attachments or links. If they want the Loom, they ask after FU2.
- No "circling back" or "just checking in." Both read as automated.
- If they say stop, stop. Mark "do not contact."
- Manual sends at first. Move a converting sequence into Instantly later, not before.

## Volume targets

- Week 1: 10 cold emails sent
- Week 2: 25 more (35 in pipeline)
- Week 3: 25 more (60 in pipeline)
- Week 4+: 25 per week minimum until you close

Most operators stall at the inbox-checking phase. The sequence runs itself once it's moving. Spend your time on the next 25, not the last 25.
