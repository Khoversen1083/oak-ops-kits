# Filled Example: Niche and ICP Decision

This is what good output looks like. It is one student's result, not a template to copy. Your niche should come from your own background and access. Use this only to calibrate the level of specificity.

---

## Interview summary (example student)

- Business model: AI automation operator, setup fee plus monthly retainer.
- Industries understood: spent 6 years in commercial HVAC, knows the trade.
- Access: still friendly with two former employers and a regional trade group.
- Painful workflows seen: dispatchers rebuilding the same schedule in spreadsheets every morning, invoices typed twice.
- Comfortable tools: spreadsheets, basic CRMs, Claude.
- Hours per week: 12.
- Geography: Phoenix metro, willing to drive 90 minutes.
- Segment preference: local trades.
- Warm contacts: 3.
- Work to avoid: anything requiring on-site hardware installs.

## Candidate niches

| Rank | Niche | Geography | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Family-owned commercial HVAC contractors | Phoenix metro | 20M to 80M | Direct experience, warm contacts, clear manual pain |
| 2 | Mechanical and plumbing contractors | Phoenix metro | 20M to 100M | Adjacent, same buyer behavior |
| 3 (backup) | Commercial landscaping firms | Phoenix metro | 15M to 60M | Seasonal scheduling pain, less personal knowledge |

## 5-test filter on Niche 1

| Test | Pass or fail | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Owner-operated | Pass | Most local HVAC shops are founder-run |
| Revenue 20M to 200M | Pass | Target band sits inside this |
| Headcount 50 to 500 | Pass | Mid-size shops run 60 to 300 |
| Some but not modern tech | Pass | Dispatch on spreadsheets, legacy field software |
| Geographic concentration | Pass | Dozens of qualifying shops in one metro |

Recommended first niche: Family-owned commercial HVAC contractors, Phoenix metro, 20M to 80M.

Why it wins: the student already speaks the language, has warm intros, and has personally watched the exact manual workflow that automation removes. Outreach credibility is high on day one.

Risks and red flags: seasonal cash flow can slow decisions in slow months; some shops were recently acquired and will not buy; avoid any shop already hiring an internal operations analyst.

Best buyer profile: owner or general manager, 45 to 60, came up as a technician, runs the business by feel, frustrated that dispatch and billing eat the first two hours of every day.

First outreach angle: "I help HVAC shops get the morning dispatch rebuild and double-entered invoicing off the owner's plate, usually 8 to 10 hours back per week."

First 3 actions this week:
1. Pull 25 qualifying shops from Sales Navigator and the trade directory.
2. Score all 25 with the ICP rubric, drop anything under 6.
3. Message the 3 warm contacts to validate the pain before cold outreach.

## Niche-specific ICP notes

- Revenue signal: cross-check the state contractor license board plus headcount times 200K.
- Tech signal: job posts mentioning "dispatch coordinator" or "data entry" score 2.
- Pain signal: owner posts about busy season, reviews mention slow scheduling.
