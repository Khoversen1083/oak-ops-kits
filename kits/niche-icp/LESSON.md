# Week 1: Pick Your Niche and Build Your First Target List

## What this kit does

This is the most important decision of your first month. This kit turns Claude into your Week 1 coach. It interviews you about your background, network, and the market you want to sell into, then it picks your niche with you, scores it against the 5-test filter, and builds your starting target list and scoring rubric. You walk away with a locked decision instead of a blank worksheet.

## What you need

- Claude Code installed and open in this folder.
- About 30 to 45 minutes.
- Optional: a LinkedIn or research tool open in another tab. The kit works without one.

## How to use it

1. Unzip this folder somewhere you can find it.
2. Open the folder in Claude Code.
3. Say: "help me get started."
4. Answer the questions one at a time. Be honest, not aspirational.
5. Review the three files it creates in the `outputs/` folder.

## What you will walk away with

Three files in `outputs/`:

- `niche-decision.md`: your 3 candidate niches, scored, with a recommended first niche, why it wins, the risks, your best buyer, your first outreach angle, and your first 3 actions this week.
- `your-icp-rubric.md`: a scoring rubric tuned to your niche, so you only email businesses worth emailing.
- `target-list.csv`: your 25-target tracker, ready to fill.

## What the outputs mean

The niche decision is your commitment for the next month. The rubric stops you from wasting outreach on bad-fit businesses. The target list is the spreadsheet you will work from every day this week. Score every target before you email anyone, and only contact the ones scoring 6 or higher out of 10.

## What to submit in Skool

Open `outputs/niche-decision.md`, copy the submit block at the bottom, and paste it into the comments on this lesson. That is your Week 1 check-in.

## Stuck?

If the kit cannot validate your niche, or you are stuck choosing between two, post your `niche-decision.md` in the community and ask for a second read. Bring your locked niche, your backup, three pain hypotheses, and three real businesses to the Week 1 live call.
