# Niche Selection Template (Week 1)

The single most important decision in your first month. The wrong niche will burn you out before you ever land a client. The right niche makes outreach 5x faster.

## The 5-test niche filter

A good niche for this work passes all five tests:

1. Owner-operated. A 50 to 60 year old founder still runs the business day to day. Not VC-backed, not private-equity rolled up.
2. Revenue band: 20M to 200M. Below 20M, no budget for a monthly retainer. Above 200M, you are competing with consulting firms.
3. Headcount: 50 to 500. Enough manual work to automate. Small enough that the owner still feels every wasted hour.
4. Industry has SOME tech in use, but not modern tech. Spreadsheets, QuickBooks, an old ERP, paper logs. They are not running Salesforce plus HubSpot plus Notion.
5. Geographic concentration is OK or preferred. Easier outreach. You can drive to one of them in 90 minutes.

## Anti-niches (do not pick)

- SaaS companies. They build their own.
- VC-backed startups. They want shiny tools, not retainers.
- Marketing agencies. They sell automation themselves.
- Solo operators and freelancers. Budget too small.
- Public sector. Procurement cycles will kill you.
- Pure tech-forward industries (crypto, AI startups). They do everything themselves.

## Pick three candidate niches

Be specific. "Construction" is too broad. "Concrete pumping companies in the Pacific Northwest, 30M to 100M, family-owned" is the right level of specificity.

## The 30-minute test

For each candidate niche, do this in 30 minutes:

1. Google `[industry] software` and `[industry] ERP`. If the top results are dated websites or Excel template marketplaces, that is a green flag.
2. Search LinkedIn for `Owner` or `President` plus `[industry]` plus `[geography]`. Can you find at least 100 profiles in 5 minutes? If yes, sourcing is doable.
3. Skim the first 5 profiles. Do they post about being slammed, working weekends, hiring problems, or operations headaches? If yes, your pain hypothesis is real.
4. Check 3 random websites in the niche. Look at the careers page (manual hiring language is a sign of automatable labor). Look at the contact page (a single email instead of a help desk usually means owner-operated, which is good).

If a niche fails the 30-minute test, drop it and pick another.

## Lock the niche

Once Niche 1 passes the 30-minute test, lock it. Stop researching. Move to the 25-target criteria.

If you cannot lock by the end of Week 1, post in the community. Do not start outreach with an unclear niche.

## What to bring to the Week 1 live call

- Your locked Niche 1
- Your backup Niche 2
- Three specific pain hypotheses for Niche 1 (for example, "weekly inventory reports take 8 hours and the ops manager dreads Mondays")
- Three names of real businesses you have found that fit
