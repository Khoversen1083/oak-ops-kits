# Setup fee anchors

The four price points inside the taught range. The decision tree
(`pricing-decision-tree.md`) tells you which cell. This file tells you
what each price point means and what the buyer is paying for at each
anchor.

Use these as the ceiling for what you commit to in scope. If you cannot
deliver what the anchor describes for the number, you are either
underpricing or overscoping.

## $1,000, the floor

When to land here:
- First paid client, you need the case study more than the margin
- Prospect is a warm referral, relationship matters more than the
  number this round
- Build is genuinely simple, one input, one output, one integration
- Prospect is under $1M revenue and benchmarks against cheap SaaS

What it covers:
- One automation, one workflow, one trigger to one output
- A single integration (one API or one source system)
- Discovery doc, build, deployment, one round of revisions before
  go-live

What it does not cover:
- Any second workflow, even if related
- Custom UI or dashboard
- Training the prospect's staff beyond a 30-minute walkthrough

Floor rule: do not go below $1,000 even for a friend. Below this you
are doing volunteer work.

## $1,500, the common starter

When to land here:
- Standard pilot for a 1-50 person business
- Light unstructured input (email parsing, simple text extraction)
- Two integrations (one source, one destination, no transforms in
  between)
- Pain is real, prospect is qualified, no rush

What it covers:
- One automation with light parsing logic
- Two integrations wired together
- Discovery doc, build, deployment, one round of revisions, 30-day
  burn-in support before retainer kicks in

What it does not cover:
- Multi-source ingestion
- Anything resembling a custom UI

## $2,000, the moderate build

When to land here:
- Prospect is 50-200 employees, or under 50 but doing $2M+ revenue
- Two or more inputs, some unstructured (calls, photos, transcripts)
- Two to three integrations with light logic in between
- Pain is acute, the prospect has tried at least one other vendor

What it covers:
- One automation with multi-source ingestion
- Two to three integrations with conditional logic
- Discovery doc, build, deployment, two rounds of revisions, 30-day
  burn-in

What it does not cover:
- A second automation, scoped separately
- Custom dashboard beyond a simple status page

## $2,500, the ceiling

When to land here:
- 50-200 person business, multi-stakeholder approval
- Multi-source, full unstructured pipeline (calls plus emails plus
  photos)
- Three or more integrations, custom logic per source
- A simple custom UI or dashboard is in scope
- Pain rated 4 or 5 out of 5, prospect is bleeding and has budget

What it covers:
- One automation, fully scoped end to end, multi-source
- Three or more integrations with custom transformation logic
- A simple status dashboard or operator UI
- Discovery doc, build, deployment, two rounds of revisions, 30-day
  burn-in

What it does not cover:
- Anything that resembles a second build (scoped separately)
- Ongoing custom development beyond the retainer adjustment

## Above $2,500

The taught range stops here. If the build genuinely needs more, that
is a sign there are two engagements hiding in one. Scope the second
one as a follow-on after Phase 1 ships. Never quote a single setup
above $2,500 inside this kit. Take the conversation to Kevin live at
`https://kevhov.ai/oak-ops` if the prospect insists on one-shot
delivery of a build that costs more.
