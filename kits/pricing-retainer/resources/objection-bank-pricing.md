# Pricing objection bank

10 pricing objections that land after the proposal is sent. One
response per objection, in builder-to-builder voice. Pre-load the
prospect name, setup fee, retainer, and workflow from
`intake-filled.md` before sending. Internalize the translation, then
say the response in your own voice. A script-read loses the room.

## 1. "Can you do it for less?"

Translation: anchoring, not a hard no on the price.

```
The price is the price. The fastest way to a lower number is a smaller
scope. Tell me which outcome from Section 3 you are willing to drop
and I will requote in 24 hours.
```

Never just lower the number. Trade scope for price every time.

## 2. "Why is the retainer so high?"

Translation: they expected a one-time fee.

```
It covers monitoring, fixes, and one adjustment per month. Without it,
you pay me hourly when something breaks, worse for you, worse for me.
The retainer means I keep it running, you do not manage me.
```

## 3. "Can we skip the retainer and just pay setup?"

Translation: they want the one-time cost only.

```
I do not do that. The automation needs a person watching it. If
[system 1] changes their API at 2am with no retainer in place, neither
of us has a clean answer for who fixes it. Either both, or I am not
the right fit.
```

Say it calmly. Do not drop it.

## 4. "We do not have budget right now."

Translation: real no budget, or pain is not acute enough yet.

```
Fair. Two questions. Does [workflow] get worse next quarter or stay
the same? And if you found another [retainer amount] somewhere, would
this be the thing you would spend it on?
```

The first answer tells you whether to follow up. The second tells you
the ranking.

## 5. "Can we start smaller?"

Translation: they want a cheaper test.

```
Yes. We scope it to just [smallest piece of workflow]. Setup drops to
$1,000, retainer drops to $300. Phase 2 adds the rest once you have
seen Phase 1 running.
```

Smaller is fine. Free is not.

## 6. "Our internal IT person can build this."

Translation: checking if they should DIY.

```
Maybe. Two questions. How loaded is your IT person, and have they
worked with [system 1] and [system 2] before? If they are slammed and
this is not their domain, you are paying weeks of their salary to do
what I would ship in two. If they have the time and the skill, hand
it over.
```

Do not fight on cost. Fight on speed and opportunity cost.

## 7. "Can we pay setup in installments?"

Translation: cash flow, not a price objection.

```
Yes. The setup fee does not change, the payment shape does. Three
equal monthly payments of [setup divided by 3], starting on signature.
Retainer starts the day after go-live as usual.
```

Setup amount stays the same. Only the payment shape moves.

## 8. "What if the automation breaks and the retainer does not cover it?"

Translation: worried about surprise invoices.

```
The retainer covers fixes for anything that breaks because of an
upstream change, an API rename, a vendor format shift. The only thing
quoted separately is net-new work, a new workflow or a new
integration. Read Section 4 of the proposal, the line is in writing.
```

## 9. "Can you guarantee a result?"

Translation: they have been burned by a vendor before.

```
I guarantee the specific outputs in Section 3 of the proposal. If by
Week 3 those outputs are not happening, I refund the setup fee. That
is the bar. I will not guarantee revenue or a business outcome we both
know is not in my hands.
```

## 10. "I need to think about it."

Translation: unclear, could be real, could be a polite no.

```
Fair. What is the specific thing you are thinking about? If it is
price, we talk scope. If it is whether [workflow] is the right first
project, that is a different conversation. I would rather know now
than chase a yes that is not there.
```

## When you hear 3 or more objections in one reply

That is a soft no. They do not want to say it.

```
Sounds like a few things are not sitting right. Honest read, is this
bad timing or the wrong project? If it is the wrong project, I would
rather know now and stay in touch when something changes. I will not
push.
```

Half the time they confess it is timing. The other half you save two
weeks of follow-up.
