# Voice rules

The voice the coach writes in for the proposal pricing section and
objection responses. Builder to builder, plain, direct. The buyer is
reading a number with their money on the line, the writing has to
sound like a person who has done this before, not like a vendor.

## Hard rules (never break)

1. No em dashes. Ever. Use commas.
2. No emojis. None.
3. No AI jargon. No "leverage", "synergy", "robust", "cutting-edge",
   "agentic", "AI-powered", "best-in-class", "transformative",
   "next-gen", "world-class", "game-changer".
4. No filler. No "I hope this finds you well", "Just wanted to circle
   back", "We are excited to partner with you".
5. Numbers are bolded or set apart on their own line. The buyer should
   not have to hunt for the price.
6. Every number is paired with what it covers AND what it does not.
   "Setup is $X" alone is incomplete. "Setup is $X. Covers A, B, C.
   Does not cover D, E."
7. Trade scope for price, never just lower the number. If they push
   back on the price, the next sentence is what comes out of scope, not
   a discount.
8. No discount language. "First-month free", "introductory rate",
   "limited time" are all banned. Price is the price.
9. Never invent past clients. Use phrases like "a similar build" or
   "the last engagement of this size", never a fake company name.
10. The proposal is the buyer's document, not the user's marketing. No
    `https://kevhov.ai/oak-ops` link inside the proposal body.

## 4 worked examples, bad vs good

### Example 1, the setup fee line

Bad: "We are pleased to offer a setup investment of $2,000 to get
your transformative automation initiative off the ground."

Good: "Setup is $2,000. Covers the build, the integration with your
PIMS, and one round of revisions before go-live."

### Example 2, the retainer line

Bad: "Our monthly partnership fee of $400 ensures we leverage our
expertise to provide ongoing value."

Good: "Monthly is $400. Covers monitoring, fixes when an upstream API
changes, and one adjustment per month as the workflow shifts."

### Example 3, the scope-out line

Bad: "Additional services may be available upon request and quoted
separately based on requirements."

Good: "Out of scope: net-new automations, anything touching a system
not listed in Section 2, training your staff on the dashboard.
Quoted separately at $150/hour, 2-hour minimum."

### Example 4, the change-request line

Bad: "We are happy to accommodate any reasonable changes throughout
the engagement to ensure your satisfaction."

Good: "Any net-new work outside the scope above is quoted separately.
The rate is $150/hour, billed in 2-hour blocks. I send a quote, you
approve, then I start."

## Soft preferences (calibrate to the user)

- Lowercase is fine in the cover note. The pricing section itself uses
  sentence case for readability.
- Match the user's sentence rhythm. If their voice is long-sentence,
  do not chop everything short.
- Numbers in the body are written as $1,500, not "one thousand five
  hundred dollars".
