# Scope control language

Verbatim phrases the coach drops into the proposal pricing section.
These are the lines that prevent the client from assuming everything
is included and asking for free work three weeks in.

Every phrase has been tested in live proposals. Copy them word for
word, swap only the bracketed placeholders.

## "What is included" section opener

> Setup covers everything in Section 2 of this proposal. That means the
> [one workflow] build, the integration with [system 1] and [system 2],
> and one round of revisions before go-live. The monthly retainer
> covers monitoring, fixes when [system 1] or [system 2] changes, and
> one small adjustment per month to the existing automation.

## "What is not included" section, paste verbatim

> Out of scope for this engagement:
>
> - Any net-new automation beyond [the one workflow]. Scoped separately.
> - Changes to systems not listed in Section 2 of this proposal.
> - Building a custom dashboard or operator UI beyond what is in
>   Section 2.
> - Training your staff on the dashboard beyond a single 30-minute
>   walkthrough at go-live.
> - Data cleanup, data migration, or historical backfill from before
>   the go-live date.
>
> Anything in the list above is quoted separately at $150/hour, billed
> in 2-hour minimum blocks. I send a quote, you approve, then I start.

## Change-request paragraph

> If the business changes and you need a new workflow automated or a
> different system integrated, that is a new engagement. I will scope
> and price it separately, the same way I scoped this one. I do not
> bundle net-new work into the retainer. This keeps the monthly fee
> predictable for both of us.

## Payment terms paragraph

> Setup is invoiced in two parts. 50 percent on signature, 50 percent
> on go-live. The monthly retainer starts the day after go-live and is
> billed monthly in advance. Net 7 on all invoices.

## Cancellation paragraph

> The retainer is month to month. You can cancel any month with 30 days
> notice. On cancellation, the automation keeps running on your
> infrastructure, you keep all the access. I stop monitoring and stop
> fixing on the cancellation date.

## "Why both numbers exist" paragraph (use only if asked)

> Setup gets the automation live. Retainer keeps it live. Without the
> retainer, the first time [system 1] changes their API or [system 2]
> renames a field, the automation breaks and neither of us has a clean
> answer for who fixes it. Either both, or this is not the right fit.

## Discount-request response (verbatim)

> The price is the price. If the number is a problem, I am happy to
> scope a smaller version. The fastest way to a lower price is a
> smaller scope, not a discount. Tell me which outcome from Section 3
> you are willing to drop and I will requote.

## Founding-customer language (use sparingly, see voice-rules.md)

If the user explicitly told you this is their first paid client of
this offer:

> First paid client of this offer rate. Setup is $1,000, retainer is
> $300. In exchange, I would ask for a written case study and a
> reference call after Month 3. Same scope as Section 2, same payment
> terms.

Never use this language for the second client. Once is a case study,
twice is a discount.
