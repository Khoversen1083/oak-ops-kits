# Price the Pilot, Set the Retainer, Send the Proposal

## What this kit does

This is the lesson that gets you paid. This kit turns Claude into your pricing coach. It interviews you about the automation you scoped, the prospect's size and budget signals, and the hours or dollars you uncovered on the discovery call. Then it lands on a setup fee inside the taught 1K to 2.5K range, a 300 to 600 per month retainer, writes a ready-to-send proposal, and arms you with the objections this specific deal is likely to hit. You walk away with a priced, scoped proposal and a retainer offer you can defend, not a blank calculator.

You also pick up a little Claude Code fluency on the way. You learn how to drive an interview, point Claude at read-only source files, and have it write finished documents into an outputs folder. That is the same loop you will run on real client builds, so the kit teaches the skill while it builds the asset.

## What you need

- Claude Code installed and open in this folder.
- About 20 to 30 minutes.
- Your discovery notes: hours saved per week, the rough hourly cost of that work, and the prospect's size. If you do not have those two numbers, you did not run a real discovery. Go back and get them first.

## How to use it

1. Unzip this folder somewhere you can find it.
2. Open the folder in Claude Code.
3. Say: "help me get started."
4. Answer the questions one at a time. Use the real numbers from your call.
5. Review the three files it creates in the `outputs/` folder.

## What you will walk away with

Three files in `outputs/`:

- `pricing-decision.md`: the scoped pilot, your setup fee with the reasoning behind it, your retainer amount, and the ROI sanity check, all inside the taught range.
- `proposal.md`: a ready-to-send proposal with the cover note, the scope, the numbers, the timeline, and what is and is not included.
- `objection-responses.md`: the 6 to 8 objections this specific deal is likely to hit, each with your response in your own voice.

## What the outputs mean

The pricing decision is the math you can defend when the prospect asks why both numbers exist. The proposal is the two-pager you send the same week, before momentum dies. The objection responses are what keep you from freezing mid-call. Price against their savings, never your time. Every setup figure stays between 1,000 and 2,500 dollars and every retainer between 300 and 600 per month, because that is the range that funds a clean build without scaring an owner-operated business.

## What to submit in Skool

Open `outputs/pricing-decision.md`, copy the submit block at the bottom, and paste it into the comments on this lesson. That is your check-in: your setup number, your retainer number, your setup-to-retainer ratio, and the ROI multiple.

## Stuck?

If the numbers feel wrong, or the ROI multiple is under 2x, or the prospect keeps pushing on the retainer and you do not have the line ready, post your `pricing-decision.md` in the community and ask for a read. Bring the deal to the live call.
