# Filled Example: Pricing, Proposal, Objections

This is what good output looks like. It is one student's result, not a template to copy. Your numbers come from your own discovery call. Use this only to calibrate the level of specificity. Any company name here is a bracketed placeholder, not a real client.

---

## Interview summary (example student)

- Scoped automation: a weekly cash-collections report that pulls overdue invoices from the bookkeeping sheet and emails the owner a clean follow-up list every Monday.
- Prospect size: small wholesale distributor, about 35 staff, no sense of revenue, pays for basic accounting software at around 50 per month.
- Manual cost: bookkeeper spends about 5 hours per week rebuilding the overdue list by hand, loaded cost about 30 per hour.
- Custom level: light unstructured, pulls from one sheet and one inbox, two systems total.
- Experience level: this is the student's first paid client, needs the case study.
- Retainer scope: monitoring and fixes, one small adjustment a month as the owner tweaks the follow-up wording.
- Budget signal: "we have never paid for anything like this."

## Pricing decision (outputs/pricing-decision.md)

Scoped automation: a weekly overdue-invoice follow-up report delivered to the owner's inbox every Monday.

Setup fee: 1,000.
Reasoning: moderate complexity at a sub-50 headcount lands the grid at the low cell, and this is a first paid client who needs the case study, so take the low end. Inside the range.

Retainer math:
- 5 hours/week x 4.3 x 0.80 = 17.2 monthly hours saved
- 17.2 x 30 = 516 monthly labor value
- 516 x 0.12 = 62
- Floored to the Starter band: 300/month.

ROI sanity check:
- Annual recovered: 5 x 30 x 52 = 7,800
- Annual retainer: 3,600
- Multiple: 7,800 / 3,600 = 2.2x
- Action: 2x to 3x band. Send the proposal, but lead with opportunity cost (the bookkeeper's Monday morning back, fewer invoices slipping past 60 days), not just the hourly math.

Tier match: the prospect has never paid for anything like this, so lead with the lowest tier and build trust. Do not over-pitch the retainer. 1,000 setup plus 300 a month is the right opening.

## Proposal (outputs/proposal.md), abbreviated

Cover note:
[firstname], here is the proposal. Quick context up top, then the scope, then the numbers.

THE PROBLEM (in your words): "Every Monday my bookkeeper burns half the morning rebuilding the overdue list by hand, and we still let invoices slip past 60 days."

THE BUSINESS CASE:
- "5 hours a week recovered means about 7,800 a year of labor cost back."
- "Net of the retainer, you net about 4,200 a year."
- "Setup pays back in under 2 months."

WHAT YOU WOULD ACTUALLY GET:
- A clean overdue-invoice follow-up list in your inbox every Monday at 7am.
- No more hand-rebuilding the list from the bookkeeping sheet.
- Fewer invoices slipping past 60 days.

(Full scope sections 1 to 9 follow, with setup 1,000 invoiced on signing and retainer 300/month on the 1st. 30-day cancellation either side.)

## Objection responses (outputs/objection-responses.md), abbreviated

"We have never paid for anything like this."
Translation: no benchmark, nervous about being first.
Response: "Totally fair, that is normal. That is why I keep the first build small. Setup is 1,000, monthly is 300, and you see the first Monday report inside three weeks. If the outputs are not landing by Week 3, I refund the setup. You are not gambling here."

"Why is the retainer so high?"
Translation: expected a one-time fee.
Response: "It covers monitoring, fixes, and one adjustment a month as your follow-up wording changes. Without it, you would call me and pay hourly the first time something broke. The 300 means I keep it running and you never manage me."

"Can we skip the retainer and just pay setup?"
Translation: wants to cut the recurring cost.
Response: "I do not do that. The report needs someone watching it. If the bookkeeping sheet format shifts and there is no retainer, neither of us has a clean answer for who fixes it. Either both, or I am not the right fit."

(Plus the first-client proof line: a sanitized live walkthrough, never a fake case study.)
