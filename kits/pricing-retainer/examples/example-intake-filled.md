# Example: intake-filled.md (worked example)

Fake niche: regional HVAC contractors, single-location, 8 to 20 trucks,
owner-operator. Used in `example-pricing-decision.md` and
`example-proposal-pricing-section.md`.

## Your business

1. **What do you sell?** A 3-week build that automates the
   service-call dispatch handoff for regional HVAC contractors.
   Replaces the dispatcher's spreadsheet plus phone-call chase to
   field techs with an automation that routes the next job from the
   CRM to the tech's phone, with the customer history attached.

2. **Who do you sell to right now?** Single-location HVAC contractors,
   8 to 20 trucks, owner-operator runs the business. Mostly suburban
   metros, $2M to $5M annual revenue.

3. **Niche this week.** HVAC contractors in the Phoenix metro.

## Your pricing history

4. **Past setup pricing.** Last three builds, $1,500, $1,800, $2,200.
   Regret the $1,500, the scope grew and I ate the overrun. Happy with
   $2,200.

5. **Past retainer pricing.** $300, $400, $500. The $300 retainer
   client churned at month 4 because I was not making time to monitor.
   The $500 client is still active 14 months later.

6. **Default change-request hourly rate.** $150/hour, 2-hour minimum
   block.

## Your current quote

7. **The workflow this build replaces.** Dispatcher's morning route
   build plus midday call chase to confirm tech ETAs and pass new
   service tickets.

8. **Prospect revenue band.** $2M to $5M.

9. **Pain rating, 1 to 5.** 4. They have already tried hiring a second
   dispatcher and it did not solve the problem.

## Operating preferences

10. **Anything else.** Owner-operator is 58, runs the business with
    his son. They benchmark against the cost of another dispatcher
    ($60K/year fully loaded). They do not benchmark against software.
    Keep "AI" out of the proposal, use "automation" or "workflow".
