# Decision tree per reply type

When a reply lands, the next 60 minutes matter. Five reply types. One
response template per type. Reply within 4 hours business hours, never
pitch in the reply, get the call first, one link and one ask per send.

## Reply type 1, interested ("Yes, let's talk" / "Tell me more, send a time")

Send the calendar link, add a prep ask. The prep ask filters
tire-kickers and pre-qualifies the call.

```
Hey <firstname>,

Great, glad it caught your attention. Here is a booking link, pick
whatever works: <calendar link>

Quick context to make the call useful, it would help to know roughly
which manual processes are slowing your team down most right now. Even
a one-liner gets us most of the way there.

Talk soon,
<your first name>
```

Status: Booked call

## Reply type 2, not now ("Not the right time" / "Q3 maybe")

Take it cleanly. Do not push. Half of these come back when the pain
gets worse.

```
Hey <firstname>,

Totally fair. If <pain area> becomes a priority, my email is the same.

Will plan to come back in 90 days with a new result in <niche>.

<your first name>
```

Status: Soft no, re-engage 90 days

## Reply type 3, send info ("What exactly do you do?" / "Send me a one pager")

Curious but cautious. Do not data-dump. Do not send the calendar link
yet. Make them confirm interest.

```
Hey <firstname>,

Short version: I find the one or two manual processes eating the most
time in a business and replace them with a custom automation. Runs in
the background, your team uses it like any internal tool, small
monthly retainer keeps it tuned as the business changes.

A recent build had a team member spending most of Mondays compiling a
status report. The automation turned that into a draft for review.

Easiest way to know if it would help <their company> is a 15 minute
call. Want me to send a booking link?

<your first name>
```

Status: Engaged, awaiting confirm

## Reply type 4, remove me ("Take me off this list" / "Unsubscribe")

Take it cleanly. Do not justify. Mark do-not-contact and move on.

```
Got it, removing you. Apologies for the noise.

<your first name>
```

Status: Hard no, do not contact

## Reply type 5, hostile ("Stop spamming me" / "This is annoying")

Same as remove me. Do not engage with the tone. Do not defend the
send. Apologize once and exit.

```
Apologies for the bother. Removing you from the list now.

<your first name>
```

Status: Hard no, do not contact

## Rules

- Reply within 4 hours business hours, same day minimum.
- Never pitch in the reply. Get the call.
- One link, one ask. If they want more, they will ask.
- Save every no with a date. Re-engage in 90 days with a new result.
- If a reply throws you, wait 6 hours and respond well. Beats replying
  fast and badly.

## What to log per reply

In the pipeline tracker, update Status to one of:

- `Booked call` (with date)
- `Engaged` (interested but not yet booked)
- `Pricing shared`
- `Soft no` (re-engage 90 days)
- `Hard no` (do not contact)
- `Routed to <new contact>` (add new row for the contact)
