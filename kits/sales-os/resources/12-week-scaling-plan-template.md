# 12-Week Scaling Plan Template

The structure Step 8 fills in for the user. Each week has 1 main goal, 1 metric, and at most 3 tasks. No optional add-ons. The plan is opinionated about what to do each week.

## Why 12 weeks

12 weeks is long enough to add 4-6 clients at a 0.5-per-week pace and short enough to actually finish. Quarterly cadence matches the cash forecast horizon and the retainer renewal cadence.

## The structure (Step 8 fills each row)

| Week | Main goal | Metric to hit | Tasks (max 3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Clean the base | Every current client audited, raise/hold/drop call made | 1. Retainer review (Step 3 output) <br> 2. Send raise emails <br> 3. Send drop emails |
| 2 | Lock the delivery template | Template v1 written and tested against last 2 builds | 1. Section 1, kickoff <br> 2. Section 2, build <br> 3. Sections 3+4, handoff and maintenance |
| 3 | Niche outreach restart | 50 new prospects in pipeline | 1. Pull 50 named accounts <br> 2. Enrich emails <br> 3. Send batch 1 |
| 4 | Discovery throughput | 3 discovery calls run | 1. Send batch 2 follow-ups <br> 2. Triage replies <br> 3. Run 3 discovery calls |
| 5 | Close 1 | 1 new client signed, kicked off using v1 template | 1. Send proposals <br> 2. Close call <br> 3. Kickoff using template |
| 6 | Capacity check-in | Re-run capacity worksheet, no surprises | 1. Hours audit <br> 2. Adjust pipeline pace if over capacity <br> 3. Decide: fire next client or hold |
| 7 | Cash check-in | Operating cash >= 3 months fixed costs | 1. Update cash forecast <br> 2. Adjust hire plan if behind <br> 3. Collect any overdue invoices |
| 8 | Outreach 2nd cycle | 50 new prospects, batch 2 sent | 1. Pull next 50 <br> 2. Reuse cold email template <br> 3. Run 3 more discovery calls |
| 9 | Close 2 | 2nd new client signed | 1. Proposals out <br> 2. Close call <br> 3. Kickoff using template v2 (after lessons from client 1) |
| 10 | Setter decision | If 5+ clients and 3+ mo cash, write setter spec (Step 6 output) | 1. Recheck cash <br> 2. Write setter spec <br> 3. Post the role |
| 11 | 3rd close window | 3rd new client signed if pipeline supports | 1. Last batch of follow-ups <br> 2. Close call <br> 3. Kickoff |
| 12 | Synthesis | Re-run Sales OS kit, output v2 plan | 1. Rerun diagnostic <br> 2. Compare actuals vs forecast <br> 3. Write next-12-week plan |

## Rules for the weekly cadence

- The metric is binary, hit or not hit. No "almost." If you did not hit it, the next week starts with a 15-minute review of why.
- Max 3 tasks. If you find yourself adding a 4th, the 4th is not happening this week, move it to next week.
- One review meeting per week with yourself, 30 minutes, Friday. Read the diagnostic, the cash forecast, the capacity plan. Are you on track?
- Skipping a week is allowed once per 12 weeks (vacation, family event). Skipping 2 weeks means the plan is wrong, rerun the Sales OS kit.

## What gets recorded each week

Append to `outputs/12-week-plan-<date>.md` as you go:

```
Week 3, 2026-06-15
Main goal: 50 new prospects in pipeline
Metric: 50 named accounts pulled, enriched, batch 1 sent
Actual: 48 pulled, 45 enriched, 45 sent
Hit? Mostly. 2 accounts had no findable email, dropped them.
Notes: Apollo had stale data on 3, switched to AnyMailFinder.
```

The log is the truth, not your memory. Read it at the week 6 and week 12 check-ins.

## How the plan adapts when reality changes

- New client closes ahead of schedule: pull the next week's outreach forward, do not coast
- New client closes behind schedule: do not panic, the model expects 0.5/week, you have buffer
- Capacity blows up (sick week, surprise scope): drop the new-client target by 1, hold the metric
- Cash dips below the 3-month floor: pause hiring decisions, run the cash forecast again
- A current client churns: the firing was right, do not chase them, fill the slot with a better client

## What the plan does NOT include

- Random calendar tasks (book the dentist, ship the wedding gift)
- Long-running side projects (the blog, the YouTube channel)
- Anyone else's work (your contractor's tasks live in their own plan)
- Nice-to-haves ("update the website") that are not on the 90-day critical path

If a task is not in the table, it is not in this quarter. Put it in a parking lot for week 13.

## When the 12 weeks end

Run the Sales OS kit again. The diagnostic will score differently, the next plan will reflect where you actually are, not where you started.
