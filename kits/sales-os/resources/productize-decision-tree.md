# Productize-or-Keep-Custom Decision Tree

When to stop customizing per client and ship a product. Reference for Step 4.

The hard take: do not productize before you have validated the niche with 3+ sold builds in it. Productizing early ships the wrong product to a niche that does not exist yet.

## The 3-build gate

You can consider productizing only if all three are true:

1. You have closed 3+ paying clients in the SAME exact niche (not "similar" niches)
2. The 3 builds reused 80 percent of the same template (you actually have a repeatable delivery)
3. At least 1 of the 3 buyers asked, unprompted, "could you turn this into a tool I can buy?"

If any are missing, do not productize yet. Keep selling custom against the template. The template IS your product foundation, but it is not the product itself.

## The decision tree

```
Have you closed 3+ paying clients in this exact niche?
  No  -> Keep custom. Run more sales cycles. Re-check in 90 days.
  Yes -> next question

Did 80 percent of the work repeat across the 3 builds?
  No  -> Keep custom. The niche may be wider than it looked. See niche-validation-5-tests.md
  Yes -> next question

Has a buyer asked, unprompted, "can I just buy this as a tool?"
  No  -> Keep custom for now. Productizing without pull will not sell. Wait for the signal.
  Yes -> next question

Do you have 6+ months of operating cash and a stable 8+ client base?
  No  -> Keep custom. Productizing eats 3-6 months of focus and cash. Do not start without runway.
  Yes -> next question

Are you willing to stop selling custom for 90 days to build the productized version?
  No  -> Keep custom. Productizing on weekends fails. It needs full attention.
  Yes -> Build the product MVP, sell to your existing client base first.
```

## When productizing makes sense (the rare yes)

- You have built the same thing 5+ times in the same niche
- Your delivery template handles 90+ percent of new builds without customization
- Buyers in your niche are explicitly asking for a self-serve version
- You have the cash to fund 90 days of product build without taking new custom clients
- You are willing to become a product company (different muscle than services)

## When productizing fails (the common no)

- The niche is too small to sustain a SaaS price point (under 200/month per buyer, you need volume you do not have)
- You have not validated the niche (1-2 paying clients is luck, not pattern)
- You productize "for scale" without buyer pull (you build, nobody buys, you go back to custom anyway)
- You try to productize while still selling custom (split focus kills both)
- You productize for the wrong reason: you are tired of clients. Productizing is not an escape from people, it is a different group of people.

## The hybrid trap

Some operators try to do both, custom delivery for big clients, self-serve product for small ones. This works at scale (8+ clients, 1+ product hires) but not for a solo operator.

If you find yourself building both at once, pick one. The custom revenue is more reliable, the product upside is bigger. Match the choice to your cash position and risk tolerance.

## What "productize" actually means at this stage

For an operator with 3-8 clients, productizing usually means one of these, not a SaaS:

1. A paid template + 1-hour onboarding call (no monthly, 500-1500 one-time)
2. A done-for-you setup + DIY maintenance docs (kill the retainer, sell more setups)
3. A group cohort that teaches buyers to install it themselves (small group, 4-8 buyers per cohort)

Real SaaS comes after 1-2 of these work, not before.

## When to revisit this decision

- Every 3 new sold builds
- Every quarterly Sales OS rerun
- When a buyer in your niche asks the productize question unprompted

Until then, keep the template, keep selling custom, keep watching for the signal.
