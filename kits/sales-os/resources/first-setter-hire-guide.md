# First Setter Hire Guide

When to hire, how to pay, where to find them, what they do, what they do not touch, and the 30-day trial structure. Reference for Step 6 of the kit.

## The hard takes (the kit enforces these)

1. Do not hire a setter before you have 5 paying clients. The setter cost will eat your cash before they generate enough booked calls to pay for themselves.
2. The first setter is part-time, commission-only. Not full-time salary. Not contractor day-rate. Commission per booked qualified call OR commission per closed deal, you pick one.
3. The setter does cold outreach and qualification only. They do not run discovery calls, write proposals, or touch delivery.
4. Run a 30-day paid trial before any longer-term arrangement.

## When to hire (the 3 triggers, all must be true)

1. You have 5+ paying clients on retainer (not setup-only)
2. You have 3+ months of fixed costs in operating cash on hand
3. You are spending 10+ hours per week on cold outreach and qualification yourself, and that is eating delivery time

If any of these is missing, do not hire. Wait. The wrong hire at the wrong time wrecks the business, not the right hire at the right time.

## Comp model (pick one)

### Option A, Per booked qualified call

- Setter is paid 50-75 per booked qualified call
- Qualified = the prospect shows up, fits the ICP, and answers the 6 discovery questions (see `../discovery-call/resources/six-question-framework.md`)
- No-show = no commission, but a credit toward the next booked call
- Cap at 20 booked calls per month, or you will incentivize spam

### Option B, Per closed deal

- Setter is paid 5-10 percent of the setup fee at close (so 100-250 per closed deal at the taught range)
- No commission on the retainer (retainer is your reward for delivery)
- Faster setter feedback loop on Option A, slower on Option B, but Option B aligns incentives better long-term

Default to Option A for the first 90 days, switch to Option B once the setter has booked 15+ qualified calls and you have closed at least 3.

## What the setter does

Week 1 to week 4:

- Pull 50 named accounts per week from your locked niche (see your niche definition in `intake-filled.md`)
- Find owner first name and email for each (use Apollo or AnyMailFinder)
- Send 50 cold emails per week using the `../cold-email/` template, scoped to your offer
- Run the 3-touch follow-up sequence
- Triage replies, route the qualified ones to your calendar
- Log every prospect into the pipeline tracker (see `../pipeline-tracker/`)

## What the setter does NOT touch

- Discovery calls (you run them, always)
- Pricing or proposals
- Delivery, kickoff, monitoring
- Refunds, scope changes, anything client-facing post-sale
- Strategy on the niche or the offer

If the setter wants to do more than cold outreach, the answer is no until they have hit 15+ qualified bookings. Then you can promote.

## Where to find them

In order of likelihood:

1. Other operators in your niche who are not yet selling on their own (a developer who is bored of dev work, an industry insider who knows the lingo)
2. SDR communities (RepVue, Bravado, but be ready to filter for the part-time commission-only model)
3. Upwork or Contra (lower bar, more filtering, set the bar with a paid test task)
4. Your own network (someone who has watched you build and wants in)

Avoid: full-time SDRs from BigCo who expect salary, "growth marketers" who do not know what cold email is, anyone who pitches themselves as a "fractional CRO."

## The 30-day paid trial

- Pay the commission as defined above for 30 days
- At day 15, check booked calls per week trend (target: 3 by end of week 2, 5 by end of week 4)
- At day 30, decide: continue, restructure, or end
- End with no drama if it is not working. The setter is paid commission they earned, no severance, no last-month, no negotiation

## What the setter needs from you to succeed

- A locked niche (Step 2 of this kit verifies this)
- A documented offer (setup fee, retainer, outcome promise)
- Access to the outreach stack (Instantly, Apollo, AnyMailFinder, calendar)
- A 30-minute weekly check-in to triage replies, calibrate voice, adjust the prospect list
- A copy of `../cold-email/CLAUDE.md` to follow your voice rules

If you cannot give them any of these, do not hire yet. Fix that first.

## What happens after a successful 30 days

- Move to month-to-month at the same comp model
- After 90 days and 15+ qualified bookings, consider switching from per-call to per-closed-deal comp
- Do not put the setter on salary until they are booking 20+ qualified calls per month consistently for 3 straight months AND you have 10+ clients
- The salary conversation is a separate kit. Not this one.
