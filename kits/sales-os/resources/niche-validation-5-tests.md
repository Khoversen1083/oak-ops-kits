# Niche Validation, the 5-Test Gate

Five tests. Each is pass or fail. No partial credit. The verdict at the end decides whether you keep the niche, narrow it, or swap it.

A niche that fails 2+ tests will not scale to 10 clients without breaking you. Better to find out at 3 clients than at 7.

## The 5 tests

### Test 1, The replicable-buyer test

Can you name 50 specific companies in this niche, by name, without thinking hard? Not "1000 vet clinics in the US," I mean by name, with the owner's first name, in your CRM or a sheet.

Pass: yes, you can pull up a list of 50 named accounts today.
Fail: you can describe the niche but you cannot point to 50 specific named companies you could outreach this week.

Why this matters: if you cannot name 50 buyers, your top-of-funnel will break at client 4 or 5. You will run out of obvious prospects.

### Test 2, The replicable-pain test

Do all of your current clients in this niche have the same top pain, in the same words? Not "they all want efficiency," I mean they all said "the morning dispatch eats 4 hours" or "I lose half my Mondays to invoicing."

Pass: 2 of 2, or 3 of 3, said the same pain in their own words at discovery.
Fail: each client had a different pain. You are not selling to a niche, you are selling to individual operators who happen to share an industry.

Why this matters: same pain means same offer, same demo, same case study. Different pain means a custom pitch every time, which does not scale past 5 clients.

### Test 3, The price-tolerance test

Are buyers in this niche paying 1K to 2.5K setup and 300-600 per month without pushback at the price reveal? Or are you constantly negotiating down to half of that?

Pass: at least 2 of your last 3 sold deals landed in the taught range without significant pushback.
Fail: you keep landing under 1K setup, or under 300 retainer, or buyers consistently push back at the reveal.

Why this matters: a niche that does not absorb the taught price range will not feed a real business. You will be working too hard for too little.

### Test 4, The repeatable-deliverable test

Could you build the same core automation for 8 of 10 buyers in this niche with 80 percent of the same code? Or is each one a different problem?

Pass: yes, you can name the 1-2 core automations that recur across this niche.
Fail: every project is structurally different. You are running a custom dev shop, not a niche operator practice.

Why this matters: same deliverable means a real template (Step 4 of this kit), which means your 5th build takes 30 percent of the time your 1st build took. Different deliverables means every build is from scratch.

### Test 5, The pull-through test

In the last 60 days, has a buyer in this niche referred you to another buyer in the same niche, unprompted? Or has a buyer in this niche come to you because they saw your work, also unprompted?

Pass: yes, at least one referral or inbound from inside the niche, no manual ask required.
Fail: every new client came from your cold outreach. Zero pull from inside the niche.

Why this matters: pull is the signal that the niche knows you exist. Without pull, every new client costs you the same outreach effort, which caps your growth at outreach throughput.

## Verdict

| Tests passed | Verdict | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 4 or 5 | Keep | Scale this niche. Run the rest of the Sales OS kit. |
| 2 or 3 | Narrow | The niche is too broad. Narrow on the dimension that pulls (size, geography, sub-vertical). Re-run this test in 60 days. |
| 0 or 1 | Swap | The niche is wrong. Stop the Sales OS kit. Open `../niche-icp/` and run that kit. Do not keep scaling a broken niche. |

## When to override the verdict

If you score "narrow" but you have 3+ paying clients and they all love you, you can keep. Document why you are overriding (it is usually that you have a sub-niche inside the niche and have not named it yet). Re-run the test against the sub-niche.

If you score "swap" but you have 1 paying client you love, the niche might still be wrong. One love-client can be luck. Two is a pattern. Three is a niche.
