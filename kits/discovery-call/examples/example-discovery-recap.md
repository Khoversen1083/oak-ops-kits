## During-call notes (the 6 questions)

### 1. Why now?

Dave: "Honestly, the LinkedIn post wasn't a joke. I lost two Monday
mornings in April rebuilding routes after we added the second yard,
and I had a board call with our biggest HOA the same week. I knew
something had to change."

Trigger: real. Spring season + second yard + visible client-facing
pressure.

### 2. Business shape

"We're at 38 staff right now, 6 crews. Two yards, Eagan and
Lakeville. Revenue's tracking to about $4.6M this year, up from $3.8M.
Mostly HOA contracts, some office parks. Snow side runs December
through March."

Size confirmed. Growth direction confirmed.

### 3. What's broken?

Dave: "The Monday morning schedule. I do it. I sit down at 6am with
the active job list, the crew availability, the weather forecast, and
I rebuild the whole week in a spreadsheet. With the second yard now I
also have to think about which crew starts where, which equipment is
at which yard. It used to take me 2 hours, now it's 5, sometimes 6."

Process named in his words: "the Monday morning schedule."
Hours confirmed: 5 to 6 per week, owner himself.

Pricing-clarity moment used: I asked "5 hours or 6," he said "5 most
weeks, 6 in peak weeks like now."

### 4. What have you tried?

"I tried ServiceTitan a couple years ago, way too expensive and too
much for what we do. Tried hiring a dispatcher last year, couldn't
find one who could actually plan routes, lasted 3 months. We use
Aspire for the job management side, that part's fine, it just doesn't
do the scheduling for us."

Competition: enterprise SaaS (too heavy), hired-and-fired ops person,
existing tool stack (Aspire stays). Budget ceiling: paid a dispatcher
roughly 60K for a quarter, so 15K in failed spend already.

### 5. What changes?

"If I got my Monday mornings back, I'd be in the field. We just lost
a 200K commercial bid because I never got the proposal out, I was
buried. The hours back is whatever, the real thing is I'd be selling
again."

VALUE HOOK in his words: "I'd be in the field" and "I'd be selling
again." Lost-bid number ($200K) is the anchor for the scope.

### 6. Who weighs in?

"Just me. My wife handles the books but she trusts me on the
operations side."

Decision-maker confirmed. No second call needed.

Dave: "Yeah, send it Tuesday, I'll look at it that night."

Email confirmed: dave@cedargrovels.com.
