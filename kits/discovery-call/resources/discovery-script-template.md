# Discovery script template

The live call script. 25 to 30 minutes, no longer. Four beats: open,
6 questions, recap, close. You keep this on screen during the call.

The script is a guide, not a teleprompter. Hit the beats in order, use
your own words. The 6 questions are the spine.

## Beat 1, Open (2 min)

Build rapport with the hook from the brief. One real observation, not
a compliment. Then set the schedule so they know what is coming.

```
[Rapport hook from brief]. How's the bandwidth on that?

[Wait for them to answer, get them talking.]

Cool, you ready to jump in?

Here's how I usually run these. First I want to hear why you booked,
what compelled you. Then I'll ask some questions about how the
business runs day to day. If I think I can help, I'll tell you how,
and we'll cover next steps. Cool?
```

Wait for the yes. Do not skip setting the schedule. It preempts the
"where is this going" tension later.

## Beat 2, The 6 questions (18 to 22 min)

Read the questions from `six-question-framework.md`. Ask one at a
time. Wait for the answer. Take visible notes. Follow up on whatever
they say is painful, do not rush to the next question.

The 6 questions are the spine. Custom follow-ups branch from each
based on what they say. Plan for 3 to 4 minutes per question.

**The pricing-clarity moment:** when they say something vague like
"most of Mondays" or "a few hours a week," stop and clarify the
number. "Most of Mondays, is that 4 hours? 6? 8?" Get the specific
number. That number is the dollar value you anchor the recap on.

## Beat 3, Recap in their words (2 to 3 min)

Before you propose anything, play their pain back to them. Use their
exact words, not your cleaned-up version. This is where the deal
either lands or breaks.

```
Okay, let me make sure I heard this right. You said [exact phrase
they used] is taking [the number you confirmed] and the reason it
hurts is [their answer to "what would change"]. Did I get that right?
```

Wait for the yes. If they correct you, write down the correction
verbatim. That correction is gold.

## Beat 4, Close to a next step (2 to 3 min)

You are NOT pitching. You are closing to a written scope, a second
call, or a proposal, whichever you committed to in intake.

Default close (written scope):

```
Here's what I'd suggest. I'll put together a tight scope, what we'd
build, the outcome, the timeline, and what it costs, and send it to
you by [specific day, 48 hours out]. Short, two pages, not twenty.

If anything I just recapped lands different than you'd say it, hit
reply and tell me. Otherwise expect the scope [day].

Sound good?
```

Confirm the email address out loud. Confirm the day. Do not promise
"this week," promise "Thursday." Specific days move deals, vague
windows kill them.

## What NOT to do on the call

- Do not quote a price. Price lands in the written scope, never on the
  call.
- Do not promise specific savings. "We'll save you 6 hours" is a
  promise you cannot keep without scoping.
- Do not name your tools. They do not care, and tool names invite
  procurement questions you cannot answer yet.
- Do not feature-dump. Discovery is the prospect talking 70 percent,
  you talking 30 percent.
- Do not run past 30 minutes. Respect the calendar block, it earns
  the next one.
- Do not fill silence after a question. Count to 5 in your head, let
  them answer.

## After the call

Hang up, write down what they said in their words, what number they
gave you, and what they said yes to at the close. Then open the
discovery-call kit and tell Claude "the call is over." You ship the
summary email inside 30 minutes.
