# The 3-sentence cold email

The first email you send. Three sentences. One specific business
problem, one concrete outcome, one ask. Owners read it on a phone in
5 seconds.

## Structure

- Subject, under 8 words, no emojis, no fake "RE:" or "quick question"
  unless the question is actually specific
- Sentence 1, hook, one specific observation about THEM, not a
  compliment, an observation
- Sentence 2, proof, one concrete result, your own or a prior buyer's
- Sentence 3, ask, calendar link OR single yes-or-no question, not both
- Signature, your first name, business email, calendar link

## Example (anonymized)

```
Subject: <prospect company> Apollo workflow

Hey <firstname>,

I noticed <prospect company> is hiring 3 SDRs and you have already
built out the Apollo plus Outreach stack, most teams your size are not
there yet.

I built a follow-up automation for a similar Series A SaaS that cut SDR
reply triage time from 4 hours a day to 30 minutes.

Worth 15 minutes this week to compare notes? <calendar link>

Kevin
kevin@kevhov.ai
```

## Variables to fill

- `<firstname>`: owner's first name. Casual is fine. Never "Mr." or
  "Ms."
- `<prospect company>`: their company name as they write it on their own
  site, not as Apollo wrote it
- `<specific observation>`: a hire, a recent post, a tool stack tell, a
  job listing. Specific enough that the email could not be sent to
  anyone else.
- `<concrete result>`: your own or a prior buyer's. Use only verified
  proof. If none yet, soft pattern: "we found a weekly reporting
  workflow that was eating hours every month."
- `<calendar link>`: your booking URL from intake

## Subject line bank

Test these in the same niche. Keep what works.

- `<firstname>, quick question on your <pain area>`
- `<Niche> founders are losing hours every week on this`
- `Saw your team is hiring for <role>, quick thought`
- `Spotted <observable thing>, quick idea`

The subject lines that reference a specific thing they posted or hired
for pull 2 to 3 times the open rate of generic openers.

## What NOT to do

- "I came across your company and was impressed by..." Every cold email
  opens this way. They have read it 50 times this morning.
- "I'd love to share how AI is transforming <industry>." They hear "AI"
  50 times a day.
- "We're a leading provider of..." They do not care.
- Naming tools. They want hours back, not technology.
- Two asks in one sentence. Pick one, calendar link OR yes-or-no
  question.
- "Just" anything. "I just wanted to..." reads as apologizing for the
  send.
- "Hope this finds you well." Cut it.
- "Circling back" or "touching base" in any sentence anywhere.

## Send mechanics

- Send Tuesday to Thursday, 7 to 9am their local time. Owner-operators
  check email early.
- Manual send at first. Move into Instantly after a sequence is
  converting, not before.
- One thread per prospect. Same thread for all follow-ups.
- No links inside the cold email body. The calendar link in the
  signature is fine. A calendar link inside the body is fine only if
  your CTA is "book a call" and you skipped the yes-or-no question.

## After sending

- Log "Sent" with date in your pipeline tracker.
- Set a Day 3 reminder for follow-up 1.
- Do not check the inbox four times an hour. Source the next 10
  targets instead.
