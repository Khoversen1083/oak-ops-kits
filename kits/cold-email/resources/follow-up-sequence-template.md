# 3-touch follow-up sequence

After the cold email on Day 0, send these three follow-ups. Then stop.
All four messages live in the same email thread. Never start a new
thread. About 5 to 10 percent of replies come from the breakup, because
owners hate to feel like they ghosted someone.

## Cadence

- Day 0, cold email v1
- Day 3, follow-up 1, concrete proof
- Day 8, follow-up 2, trade the ask
- Day 14, follow-up 3, the breakup
- Day 14 and after, stop. Re-engage in 90 days only if you have
  shipped a relevant new result.

## Follow-up 1 (Day 3)

```
Hey <firstname>,

Following up on the note below. Realized I should have included a
quick example.

Same kind of <niche> business, <headcount range> team. They had
<specific manual process> eating about <time per week>. Built <one-line
automation>, now it runs overnight and the team gets the output by
7am.

Worth a 15 minute call?

<your first name>
```

Why this works: the cold email pitched the outcome. Follow-up 1 makes
it concrete with a specific case. Same thread, replies to your own
original send.

## Follow-up 2 (Day 8)

```
Hey <firstname>,

Quick second nudge. Curious if <pain hypothesis> is actually a thing
at <their company>, or if I am off base.

If it is, I can put together a 30 second walkthrough showing exactly
what we would build before any call. If it is not, what is actually
eating your team's time these days?

<your first name>
```

Why this works: trades the ask. Instead of demanding their time, asks
them to confirm or correct your hypothesis. The 30 second walkthrough
is a low-friction offer of value. If they reply "actually X is the
bigger problem" you have new information, update Follow-up 3 to
reference X.

## Follow-up 3, the breakup (Day 14)

```
Hey <firstname>,

Last note from me, I do not want to clog your inbox.

If <pain area> is not a priority right now, totally fair. If it
becomes one, my email is the same. Will plan to come back in a few
months when we have more results in <niche>.

<your first name>
```

Why this works: removes the pressure and tells them you are moving on.
The clean exit is what triggers the reply, owners do not want to feel
like they ghosted someone who was respectful.

## Rules

- Same thread every touch. Never start a new one.
- No new attachments or links. If they want the 30 second walkthrough,
  they ask after Follow-up 2.
- Never write "circling back" or "just checking in." Both read as
  automated.
- If they say stop, stop. Mark "do not contact" in your tracker.
- Manual sends at first. Move a converting sequence into Instantly
  later, not before.

## What to log per touch

| Touch | Sent date | Replied? | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 0 cold | | | Awaiting reply |
| Day 3 FU1 | | | Awaiting reply |
| Day 8 FU2 | | | Awaiting reply |
| Day 14 FU3 | | | Closed, no response |

If they reply at any point, switch Status to one of: Booked call,
Asked for info, Soft no, Hard no, Wrong contact. Then stop the cadence
and switch to the reply decision tree.

## When to break the cadence

If they reply but do not book, switch to
`decision-tree-replies.md`. Do not continue the cold sequence on top of
a real conversation.

## Volume targets

- Week 1, 10 cold emails sent
- Week 2, 25 more, 35 in pipeline
- Week 3, 25 more, 60 in pipeline
- Week 4 and after, 25 per week minimum until you close

Most operators stall at the inbox-checking phase. The sequence runs
itself once it is moving. Spend your time on the next 25, not the last
25.
