# The weekly anchor piece

The one substantial piece you publish each week. Everything else for
the week is chopped from this. The anchor is either a LinkedIn post
(150 to 250 words) or a Loom video (3 to 5 minutes, 5 beats outline).
Pick one format per week, do not split your effort.

## Structure (LinkedIn post)

- Hook, one specific moment or observation from the last 7 days
- Build, the pattern or workflow that observation revealed, in plain
  English
- Proof, one concrete number, name, or before-and-after detail
- Soft close, names who this is for, no hard CTA

## Structure (Loom video, 5 beats)

- Beat 1, 20 seconds, the moment that made you record this
- Beat 2, 45 seconds, the build or pattern, walked through
- Beat 3, 60 seconds, the proof, ideally on screen
- Beat 4, 45 seconds, who this is for and why
- Beat 5, 30 seconds, where to go next, never a hard pitch

## Example (LinkedIn anchor, anonymized)

```
Spent 40 minutes Sunday night writing one LinkedIn post. Deleted it.

The post was generic, three takeaways about content strategy. It
could have been written by anyone. By Tuesday morning I was back to
zero.

Switched the workflow. Now the anchor gets drafted Friday afternoon
right after my last client call, while the actual work is still in
my head. Pulled the last 6 anchors into a sheet to check. Three of
them came from the same 15 minute call transcript.

The pattern, I was trying to write about my work instead of from
inside it. The fix was timing, not effort.

If you are a solo operator writing your own posts and Sunday night
keeps eating the draft, try moving it to Friday, right after the
client call, before the week ends.
```

## Variables to fill

- `<specific moment>`: a real thing that happened this week. A call,
  a build, a thing a client said, a thing you noticed in your own
  workflow. Specific enough that only you could write it.
- `<the pattern>`: the underlying lesson the moment revealed. One
  sentence. Do not lead with this.
- `<concrete proof>`: a number, a name, a sheet count, a time delta.
  Real. Never invented.
- `<who this is for>`: the exact buyer this lands for. "Vet clinic
  owners writing their own posts." Not "founders" or "operators".

## What NOT to do

- Do not open with the lesson. "Three things I learned this week about
  content" buries the only reason a reader stops scrolling.
- Do not stack 7 bullet points. One observation, one build, one proof,
  one close.
- Do not write a CTA into the anchor. The CTA lives on the offer
  sheet, never in the post.
- Do not name a tool. "I built it in Claude Code" pulls the reader
  toward the tool. "I built a follow-up automation" keeps them on the
  outcome.
- Do not use a stock photo. If the post needs a visual, use a
  screenshot of the actual thing you built or the actual sheet you
  pulled.

## Cadence

- One anchor per week, posted Tuesday or Wednesday morning local time
- Drafted the prior Friday, edited Monday, posted Tuesday
- Same surface every week for the first 12 weeks, do not platform-hop

## After publishing

- Log it in the content calendar with the date and surface
- Chop the 3 derivatives same day (see `derivatives-template.md`)
- Watch the comments for 48 hours, reply to every comment in the
  first 24 hours, that is where the inbound DMs start
- Note any prospect who comments or DMs in the pipeline tracker

## When the anchor flops

Most anchors that flop are missing the proof line. The hook was good,
the build was clear, but there was no concrete number or detail. Re-
read your draft. If you cannot point at one sentence that has a
number, a name, or a before-and-after, the post is too abstract.
Rewrite that sentence first.
