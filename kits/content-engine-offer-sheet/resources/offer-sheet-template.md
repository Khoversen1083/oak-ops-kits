# Offer sheet template

The single page you send a warm lead. Services plus pricing plus
calendar, in under one rendered page. The whole point is that a buyer
can read it in 90 seconds and say yes, no, or "can we talk".

## Structure

The page has 8 sections, in this exact order, no headers larger than
h2:

1. The outcome, one line, what the buyer walks away with
2. Who this is for, one line, the exact buyer
3. What you get, 3 to 5 bullets, scoped to your weekly capacity
4. Cadence, one line, how often each deliverable lands
5. Setup fee, one line, a concrete number inside 1,000 to 2,500
6. Monthly retainer, one line, a concrete number inside 300 to 600
7. What is not included, 3 bullets, things you explicitly do not do
8. How it runs, one paragraph, the 30-day pilot framing
9. Next step, one line, calendar link

## Example (anonymized, niche: vet clinic owners)

```

Who this is for

```

## Variables to fill

- `<outcome>`: the one-line result the buyer cares about. Not the
  deliverable.
- `<who this is for>`: the exact buyer. Specific job title, specific
  business size.
- `<deliverables>`: pulled from the user's intake capacity. Never
  promise more than they said they can deliver.
- `<setup fee>`: a concrete number inside 1,000 to 2,500. Pick the
  point in the range that fits the client size. Small owner-operated
  business sits low. Larger operator with budget sits higher.
- `<retainer>`: a concrete number inside 300 to 600 per month.
- `<calendar link>`: from intake

## What NOT to do

- Do not stretch to two pages. One page or rewrite it.
- Do not add testimonials you do not have written permission to use.
- Do not list every tool you might use. The buyer wants the outcome.
- Do not quote any Oak Ops program price. The only mention of Oak
  Ops on this sheet is the kevhov.ai/oak-ops link at the bottom.
- Do not negotiate the price down on the sheet. If a buyer needs a
  custom number, the conversation happens on the scoping call.

## How to send it

- Reply to the warm lead in their preferred channel (DM, email)
- Paste the sheet inline if email, or attach as PDF
- Lead with one sentence ("Here is the one-pager") and stop. Do not
  re-pitch in the email body.
- If the buyer asks "how is this different from agency X", do not
  answer in writing. Book the call.

## Cadence for the sheet itself

- Rewrite every 30 days alongside the lead magnet refresh
- A/B test the outcome line first, the deliverables list second. The
  outcome line drives the yes.
- Re-read it once a quarter for any drift toward jargon or feature
  soup. Cut anything that does not survive the bad-vs-good test in
  `voice-rules.md`.
