# Derivatives template

After the anchor lands, chop it into 3 short-form posts for your
secondary surfaces. Each derivative pulls from one piece of the anchor.
Never copy-paste the whole thing across surfaces, the algorithms
punish that and the reader checks out.

## The 3 chops

### Derivative 1, the hook alone

The opening line or moment from the anchor, standalone. No build, no
proof, no close.

- Surface: Twitter, IG caption, Threads
- Length: under 280 characters, one sentence ideal
- Day: same day as the anchor, 4 hours later

Example, pulled from the anchor in `anchor-piece-template.md`:

```
Spent 40 minutes Sunday night writing one LinkedIn post. Deleted it.
```

### Derivative 2, the proof or number

The concrete number, name, or before-and-after from the middle of the
anchor. Posted as a one-line callout or a single screenshot.

- Surface: IG reel cover, Twitter, LinkedIn comment on someone else's
  post
- Length: one line plus optional screenshot
- Day: Day 2 after the anchor

Example:

```
Pulled the last 6 LinkedIn anchors into a sheet. 3 of them came from
the same 15 minute client call transcript.
```

### Derivative 3, the close or "who this is for"

The closing line from the anchor, turned into a question back at the
audience. Invites a comment, no hard CTA.

- Surface: Threads, Twitter, IG story
- Length: under 280 characters, ends in a question
- Day: Day 3 after the anchor

Example:

```
Solo operators writing your own posts, when does your draft actually
get written? Sunday night, or right after a client call?
```

## Why this works

The anchor earns the reader's full attention once. The derivatives
catch the same reader (or a new one) 2 to 3 more times on different
surfaces, with different framings of the same idea. The hook lands
people in your world. The proof builds trust. The question starts the
DM thread.

## Variables to fill

- `<hook>`: the first line of the anchor
- `<proof>`: the one sentence in the anchor with a number, name, or
  before-and-after
- `<close>`: the last line of the anchor, turned into a question

## What NOT to do

- Do not repost the entire anchor on a second surface. Chop it.
- Do not write a thread. Threads dilute the one good idea into 6
  mediocre lines. One sentence per derivative.
- Do not add a CTA to the derivatives. The CTA lives on the offer
  sheet, the offer sheet is linked from your bio.
- Do not schedule all 3 derivatives at the same time. Space them
  across 3 days so each one gets its own air.

## Cadence

- Derivative 1, same day as anchor, 4 hours later
- Derivative 2, Day 2
- Derivative 3, Day 3
- Day 4 to Day 7, do not post. Reply to comments and DMs.

## After publishing

- Log each derivative in the content calendar with date and surface
- Track which derivative drove the most DMs into the pipeline
  tracker. After 4 weeks you will know which chop format your
  audience prefers, lean into it.
