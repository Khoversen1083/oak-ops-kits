# Lead magnet template

One monthly lead magnet that solves one specific pain for your niche.
Refreshed every 30 days so it stays current. Routes back to the
matching paid kit (cold-email, discovery-call, or other) or to your
calendar.

## Structure

A one-page PDF or Notion doc with 5 to 7 sections, in this order:

1. The promise, one line, what the reader walks away with
2. Who this is for, one line, the exact buyer
3. The pain, 2 to 3 sentences naming the specific friction
4. The fix, the actual playbook, 3 to 5 steps with examples
5. The pitfall, the one mistake most people make when running it
6. The next step, one CTA, either the matching kit or the calendar
7. About, 2 lines on who wrote it and why they wrote it

## Example outline (niche: vet clinic owners)

```
Promise: A 4-step morning routine that cuts front-desk reminder
calls from 6 hours a week to 30 minutes.

Who this is for: Owner-vets running single-location clinics, 2 to 6
vets, where the front desk still calls every appointment to confirm.

The pain: Front desk burns the first hour of every morning on
reminder calls. Half the clients do not answer. The notes get lost
between the call sheet and the PIMS. By lunch the team is behind.

The fix:
  1. Pull tomorrow's schedule from the PIMS into a sheet every
     evening at 6pm
  2. Auto-send a text reminder to every confirmed appointment at 7am
  3. Route the responses into the front desk inbox with a label
  4. Front desk handles only the exceptions, the rest are silently
     confirmed

The pitfall: Most clinics try to add a tool on top instead of
replacing the routine. The tool gets ignored within 3 weeks. The
fix is to delete the call-list step, not augment it.

Next step: If you want to see the actual workflow run on your
schedule, book 15 minutes here. <calendar link>

About: Built by an operator who has shipped this exact workflow for
3 clinics. Plain English, no jargon.
```

## Refresh checklist (monthly)

Run this on the first business day of every month:

- Re-read the magnet end to end. Does the pain still match what you
  hear on calls?
- Update one number, name, or example with something from the last
  30 days
- Check the CTA link, the calendar URL or kit reference, still works
- Update the file name with the new month (e.g.
  `vet-reminder-playbook-2026-06.pdf`)
- Repost the lead magnet to your bio link and pin it on your primary
  surface

## What NOT to do

- Do not write a 20-page ebook. One page wins. Two pages is the cap.
- Do not gate it behind a 7-field form. Email and first name max.
- Do not use stock illustrations. A screenshot of the actual workflow
  or sheet beats every illustration.
- Do not promise a result you have not delivered. "Cuts 6 hours a
  week" only if you actually shipped it.
- Do not put the Oak Ops program price anywhere on the magnet. The
  CTA routes to your calendar or to https://kevhov.ai/oak-ops only.

## How it ties back to the rest of the engine

The lead magnet is the trade. The reader gives you their email, you
give them the one-page fix. The follow-up email (drafted in the
cold-email kit) lands within 24 hours and invites them to a call. The
call is run with the discovery-call kit. The offer sheet (in this
kit) is the document you send after the call.

Each kit hands off to the next. The lead magnet starts the chain.
