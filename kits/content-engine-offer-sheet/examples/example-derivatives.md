# Example: derivatives-2026-05-30.md (worked example)

Generated for the example anchor (`example-anchor-piece.md`). 3 chops,
spaced over 3 days across the user's secondary surfaces (Threads
first, Twitter second).

---

## Derivative 1, the hook alone

- Surface: Threads
- Day: Tuesday, 4 hours after the LinkedIn anchor lands
- Length: 119 characters

```
Spent 40 minutes Sunday night writing one LinkedIn post for a vet
clinic client. Deleted it.
```

Notes: just the hook from the anchor, standalone. No build, no
context. If a reader stops on this, they will click the profile, find
the anchor, and read the rest. That is the job.

---

## Derivative 2, the proof or number

- Surface: Twitter
- Day: Wednesday, mid-morning
- Length: 158 characters

```
Pulled the last 6 LinkedIn anchors I wrote for vet clinic clients
into a sheet. 4 of them came from the same 20 minute call with one
owner-vet in Madison.
```

Notes: the proof sentence lifted from the middle of the anchor,
turned into a standalone observation. A reader who skipped the
anchor still gets the punchline.

---

## Derivative 3, the close or "who this is for"

- Surface: Threads
- Day: Thursday, end of day
- Length: 175 characters

```
Owner-vets writing your own LinkedIn posts, when does the draft
actually get written? Sunday night, or Friday right after your last
appointment? Curious which one wins for you.
```

Notes: the close from the anchor, turned into a question. Invites a
reply. Comments on this kind of post are where the first DMs start.

## Pre-post checklist for derivatives

- Confirm each derivative is under 280 characters.
- Confirm no hard CTA in any derivative. The CTA lives on the offer
  sheet.
- Confirm the 3 derivatives do not say the same thing 3 ways. Hook,
  proof, question are 3 different shapes.
- Log each post in `outputs/content-calendar-<date>.md` after it
  ships.
