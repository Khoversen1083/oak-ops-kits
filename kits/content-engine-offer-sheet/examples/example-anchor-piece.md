```
Spent 40 minutes Sunday night writing one LinkedIn post for a vet
clinic client. Deleted it.

The post was generic, three takeaways about "modern clinic
operations". It could have been written by anyone. By Tuesday morning
the draft was already stale and I was back to staring at a blank
editor.

Switched the workflow. Now the anchor gets drafted Friday afternoon
right after my last clinic call, while the actual work and the
client's exact words are still in my head. Pulled the last 6 vet
clinic posts into a sheet to check. 4 of them came from a single 20
minute call with one owner-vet in Madison, the one where she walked
me through her morning reminder-call routine.

The pattern, I was trying to write about my work instead of from
inside it. The fix was timing, not effort. Friday writing pulls from
a hot memory. Sunday writing pulls from nothing.

If you are an owner-vet writing your own posts and the Sunday night
draft keeps getting deleted, try moving it to Friday, right after
your last appointment, before the week ends and the details fade.
```

  reader can verify against their own process
- Soft close names the exact buyer (owner-vet writing their own
  posts) without a hard CTA
- The anchor is 207 words, inside the 150 to 250 target
- No em dashes. Commas only. No emojis. No "AI" or "agents"
