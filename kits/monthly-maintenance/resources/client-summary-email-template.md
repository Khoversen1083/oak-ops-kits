# Client summary email template

The monthly email you send the buyer. Plain language, no jargon, no
tool names unless the buyer already uses them. Three sections, five
sentences or fewer per section. The buyer reads it on a phone in 30
seconds and knows you are still worth the retainer.

## Structure

- Subject, under 10 words, includes the month
- Greeting, first name only, lowercase fine if the relationship is
  casual
- Section 1, what ran this month, the numbers the buyer cares about
- Section 2, what we improved, one or two real changes in plain words
- Section 3, what is next, one or two specific things coming in the
  next 30 days
- Signoff, your first name, no calendar link unless they asked

## Example (anonymized)

```
Subject: May recap, reminder workflow

Hey Sarah,

This month the reminder workflow ran 1,240 times, up from 980 in
April when you launched the dental campaign. Two failed runs both
caused by a data field that got renamed in the practice management
system, fixed within an hour.

Shipped a small change so the workflow now flags any patient with a
no-show in the past 30 days, the front desk can give them a personal
call instead of an automated reminder.

Next month I will add a weekly summary email so you see runs and
exceptions without logging into anything. Also planning to test a
post-visit follow-up tweak for surgical cases, will share a draft
before turning it on.

Jordan
```

## Variables to fill

- `<month>`: month being recapped, written out (May, not 05)
- `<firstname>`: buyer's first name from intake
- `<run count>`: from the audit, Section 2
- `<comparison>`: up, down, flat versus last month, one phrase
- `<incidents>`: from Section 1, if any
- `<improvement>`: from Section 7, written in buyer words
- `<next month>`: from Section 8, one or two specific items

## What NOT to do

- Do not paste log lines, error codes, or tool names the buyer does
  not already use.
- Do not list every single thing you did. Three sections, that is it.
- Do not pitch the upsell in this email. The upsell goes in a separate
  send, after the recap has landed.
- Do not say "leverage", "robust", "best-in-class", "synergy",
  "transformative".
- Do not write "just wanted to send the monthly recap." Send it.
- Do not include the kevhov.ai link in the buyer email. That link is
  for your own marketing.

## Send mechanics

- Send within 2 business days of running the audit. Same day is
  better.
- BCC your own internal tracker so the email is logged.
- Calendar a 15 minute follow-up for the buyer 5 to 7 days after the
  send, only if you want to talk about the upsell from the internal
  plan.

## Pre-send checklist

- Three sections, under 5 sentences each. Confirmed.
- No tool names the buyer does not already use. Confirmed.
- No jargon, no em dashes, no emojis. Confirmed.
- Run count and any incidents match the audit doc exactly.
- Subject line includes the month.
