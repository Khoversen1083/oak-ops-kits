# Upsell trigger checklist

The signals that say a client is ready for a second build or a higher
retainer. Run this checklist against every monthly audit. For each
trigger that fired, write the one-line pitch the user can send the
client 5 to 7 days after the recap email.

Most retainer growth comes from existing clients, not new logos. Miss
the trigger, miss the sale.

## The 8 triggers

### Trigger 1, the client asked for a second workflow

They mentioned, in passing or directly, another manual process eating
their team's time. Even one mention in a single month is a trigger,
two in two months is hot.

Pitch shape: "The <workflow they mentioned>, I can scope a build for
it next week if you want a quick proposal."

### Trigger 2, the current automation hit its value ceiling

The original workflow is humming, the metric is flat, the team has
absorbed the time savings. They are ready to point the next problem at
you.

Pitch shape: "Now that <current workflow> is running on its own,
worth 15 minutes to look at the next biggest time sink on the team's
calendar?"

### Trigger 3, their team grew

They hired in the last 30 to 60 days. New hires bring new manual
workflows and the buyer is in spending mode.

Pitch shape: "Saw you brought on <role>, common pattern is the
<related manual workflow> gets messy at this stage, want me to take a
look?"

### Trigger 4, they hit a busy season

Their business is in peak season. The current automation kept them
upright, the team is feeling the pain of the workflows that are still
manual.

Pitch shape: "<Busy season> is going to hit again next quarter, want
me to scope what we should automate before it does?"

### Trigger 5, they referred someone to you

A buyer who refers is a buyer who would happily buy more from you. The
referral is the strongest possible signal.

Pitch shape: "Appreciate the intro to <referred company>. While we
are in the loop, anything on your side you want me to look at for
next month?"

### Trigger 6, they asked for a custom report or dashboard

They want visibility into what the automation is doing. That visibility
is its own deliverable, scoped and priced separately from the build.

Pitch shape: "I can build a small dashboard that gives you <metric
they asked about> on its own, one-time build plus a small bump to the
retainer."

### Trigger 7, the team complained about a manual workflow

Buyer reported a team frustration to you in passing. The buyer is
already framing the problem as "we need to fix this."

Pitch shape: "On the <workflow the team complained about>, that is
almost always the next thing to automate at your stage, want me to
scope it?"

### Trigger 8, they paid the invoice early or without question

Cash flow is healthy and you are not on a price battle. The price
sensitivity is low this month, a good moment to introduce the next
build.

Pitch shape: combine with any other trigger that fired. Do not pitch
on this trigger alone, use it as the green light to send a pitch you
were already considering.

## How to use it

- After the audit, walk through all 8 triggers. Mark each fired or not
  fired.
- For every fired trigger, write the one-line pitch into the internal
  next-month plan.
- Send the pitch 5 to 7 days after the recap email, in a separate
  thread, with a clear subject like "<client>, quick idea on <thing>".
- If no triggers fired this month, do not force a pitch. Wait until
  they fire. Forcing a pitch breaks trust.

## Rules

- One pitch per month per client. Two is too many.
- Tie every pitch to a real trigger. No pitching to hit a quota.
- If a pitch lands a yes, the build goes through the same intake the
  original did. New scope, new SOW, new start date.
- If 3 monthly cycles pass with zero triggers and zero pitches, the
  retainer is at risk of going stale. Raise it as a conversation,
  not as a pitch.
